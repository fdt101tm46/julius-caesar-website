Biography

The Man Who
Shaped an Empire

Gaius Julius Caesar was born on 12 July 100 BC into a patrician family of Rome. Though his family claimed divine descent from Venus through Aeneas, Caesar grew up in the Subura — one of Rome's rougher neighbourhoods — grounding him in the realities of ordinary Roman life.

His career was defined by ambition, military genius, and political cunning. After serving as a general in the Gallic Wars from 58 to 50 BC, he returned to Rome in defiance of the Senate, crossing the Rubicon with his army in 49 BC — a moment that triggered civil war and forever changed the Republic.

He was appointed Dictator perpetuo — dictator in perpetuity — in early 44 BC. On the Ides of March, 15 March 44 BC, he was assassinated by a group of senators, led by Brutus and Cassius, who feared the end of the Republic.
