Biography

Who Was Julius Caesar?

Gaius Julius Caesar was born on 12 July 100 BC into the patrician gens Julia — an ancient Roman clan that traced its lineage to the goddess Venus and the Trojan hero Aeneas. Raised in the Subura, one of Rome's most densely populated districts, Caesar's early life was shaped by political upheaval. His father, a praetor and governor, died suddenly when Caesar was only sixteen, thrusting the young man into early political manoeuvring.

Caesar's formative years forged a man of extraordinary range. He possessed a brilliant intellect that mastered philosophy, rhetoric, and military theory with equal facility. He studied under the finest teachers in Rome and later in Rhodes, deepening his command of Greek literature and oratory. His ambition was matched only by his gift for winning loyalty — soldiers adored him, crowds cheered him, and senators feared what he might become.

From proconsul to conqueror, from consul to dictator, Caesar's rise reordered the Roman world. By 44 BC he held the title Dictator Perpetuo. His reforms of the calendar, the Senate, the law, and the city of Rome itself outlasted the Republic he had dissolved. He became, in death, the symbol of absolute rule: every Roman emperor, every German Kaiser, every Russian Tsar borrowed his name.

Life & Career

The Timeline of Caesar

100 BC

Birth in Rome

Born 12 July into patrician gens Julia in the Subura district. Family claimed descent from Venus through Aeneas.

84 BC

Marriage to Cornelia

Married Cornelia, daughter of populist leader Cinna. Alliance with popular faction put Caesar at odds with Sulla.

81 BC

Military Service in Asia Minor

Served under Marcus Minucius Thermus, earned the Civic Crown for bravery at the siege of Mytilene.

73 BC

Elected Military Tribune

Began political career using charm, oratory, and lavish spending. Known for generosity to soldiers and citizens.

63 BC

Elected Pontifex Maximus

Won prestigious position of chief priest of Rome, a massive political victory against older, established rivals.

60 BC

First Triumvirate

Secret political alliance with Crassus and Pompey — unofficial ruling coalition for nearly a decade.

58–50 BC

The Gallic Wars

Eight gruelling years of campaigns across Gaul. Commentarii de Bello Gallico remains a masterpiece of military writing.

49 BC

Crossing the Rubicon

"Alea iacta est." Caesar led his Thirteenth Legion across the Rubicon, igniting the Roman Civil War.

48 BC

Defeat of Pompey at Pharsalus

Decisively defeated former ally Pompey in Greece, making him master of the Roman world.

46 BC

Appointed Dictator

Named Dictator for ten years, later Dictator Perpetuo. Began sweeping reforms of law, Senate, and calendar.

46 BC

The Julian Calendar Reform

Introduced 365.25-day solar calendar, replacing Rome's chaotic lunar system. Foundation of our modern calendar.

44 BC

Assassination — The Ides of March

15 March 44 BC, senators led by Brutus and Cassius stabbed Caesar 23 times. "Et tu, Brute?"
